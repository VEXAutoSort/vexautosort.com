About VEXAutoSort

Build season buries you in tiny parts. So we built a robot to dig you out.

Every VEX team loses hours sorting screws, nuts, and spacers by hand. VEXAutoSort is a robotic arm that learns the job by watching a person, then does it on its own.

We don't write a rule for every part. We show the arm how to sort by hand and it copies us. Simple 3D-printed sorters mean it only ever has to learn one move: pick a part up, drop it in one spot.

We start with two parts that look nothing alike, get that working, then add one type at a time.

What we offer

A system any team can build from scratch.

01

It learns, you don't program it

The arm copies what a person shows it, so there's no fiddling with motion code every time you add a new part.

02

Smart mechanical shortcuts

A few 3D-printed sorters turn a hard "which bin?" question into one simple pick-and-place the arm can nail.

03

Two cameras, better aim

An overhead camera and a wrist camera give the arm the depth and context to grab small parts reliably.

04

Made to be copied

Clear docs and a parts list mean another team can build the whole thing themselves.
