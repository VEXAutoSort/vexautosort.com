About VexSort

Build season buries you in tiny parts. So we built a robot to dig you out.

Every VEX team loses hours sorting screws, nuts, and spacers by hand. VexSort is a robotic arm that learns the job by watching a person, then does it on its own.

We don't code a rule for every part. We just show the arm how to sort by hand, and it copies us. Simple 3D-printed sorters mean it only ever has to learn one move: pick a part up, drop it in one spot.

We start with two parts that look nothing alike, get it working, then add one type at a time.

What we offer

Not just a demo — a system anyone can rebuild.

01

It learns, you don't program it

The arm copies what a person shows it, so there's no fiddling with motion code for every new part.

02

Smart mechanical shortcuts

A few 3D-printed sorters turn a hard "which bin?" problem into one simple pick-and-place the arm can nail.

03

Two cameras, better aim

An overhead camera and a wrist camera together give the arm the depth and context to grab small parts reliably.

04

Made to be copied

Clear docs and a parts list mean another team can build the whole thing from scratch.
