The journey

From our own bins to every team's bench.

How VexSort goes from a fix for our own sorting headaches to something any VEX team can build and run.

Phase 0 The problem & the spark · June 2026
Pre-season · VEX Worlds 2026

The spark

The idea hit during manual sorting at the World Championships. Every team in the room was losing the same hours to the same tedious job — a problem worth solving once, for everyone.

Early June

A nudge from the pros

A chat with the CTO of a robotics company pointed us toward the SO-ARM101 and an approach where the robot learns by copying a human. They also warned us about the hard part: getting it reliable across 50-plus different parts.

Jun 7

The mechanical breakthrough

The insight that makes this shippable: don't make the robot classify every part. Pair it with simple 3D-printed sorters so the arm only learns one motion — and the design stays cheap and easy to reproduce.

Jun 8

Hardware online

Got the arm initialized, calibrated, and running in teleoperation. Added the first camera. The platform other teams would eventually replicate was now in our hands.

Phase 1 Prove it works · Jun–Jul 2026
Late June

Custom gripper + cameras

Design and print a gripper tip for tiny parts, mount wrist and overhead cameras, and lock in consistent lighting — all parts that have to be reproducible from a parts list.

Early–mid July

First demonstrations

Pick the two most distinct parts (screws vs. standoffs) and collect ~150–200 teleoperation demos, verifying data quality as we go.

Late July

First trained policy

Train the first ACT policy, document every failure mode on video, collect targeted demos to patch the worst ones, and retrain until the two-part sort is reliable.

Phase 2 Make it general · Aug–Nov 2026
Fall

From 2 parts to the full bin

Scale to five-plus part types at high reliability, tune cameras and lighting to fix visual confusions, and compare policy types — the breadth a real team's parts bin demands.

Late fall

Lock the reference design

Freeze the hardware layout, parts list, and configuration into a single known-good setup — the thing a second team will build from.

Phase 3 Package it for others · Late 2026
Winter

Docs anyone can follow

Write step-by-step build and setup guides, record clear demo videos, and clean up the codebase so a team with no ML background can get sorting.

Winter

Bill of materials & cost

Cost out every component and target a price point that's realistic for a high school team's budget.

Phase 4 Get it into teams' hands · 2027
Early 2027

Pilot with real teams

Get one or two other VEX teams to build VexSort using only our docs. Wherever they get stuck is what we fix next.

2027

Decide how to ship

Open-source the design, sell ready-to-run kits, or both. The goal: any team that wants to stop sorting by hand can.
